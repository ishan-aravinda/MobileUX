About Us

We own and operate the railway infrastructure in England on behalf of the nation. That's 20,000 miles of track, 30,000 bridges, tunnels and viaducts and the thousands of signals, level crossings and stations. We also manage rail timetabling and 10 of the largest stations in England. We work round-the-clock to provide a safe, reliable experience for the millions using Europe's fastest-growing railway each and every day. The work we do in running and improving Britain's railway is incredibly diverse, challenging and ambitious. We work with lots of different partners across the industry, so why not know a little more about who work collaboratively to deliver 4.8 million journeys a day for our customers.


Our Team

Maintaining and operating our rail network 24/7 is an important part of what we do, but we also plan ahead and continually improve the way we work. We keep Britain moving. The railway brings together families and friends, and opens up communities to opportunities: jobs; clients and customers. It helps businesses and the national economy to grow, and it's our job to look after it.
